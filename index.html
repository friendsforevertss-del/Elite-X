Types of Fake News

Clickbait

Sensational headlines designed to attract attention and generate clicks, often with misleading content.

Propaganda

Biased or misleading information used to promote a particular political cause or point of view.

Satire/Parody

Content that mimics news but is intended for humor or commentary, often mistaken for real news.

Outdated Content

Old news stories presented as current events, causing confusion about their relevance.

Imposter Content

False information from sources that appear legitimate but are actually fake or manipulated.

Manipulated Content

Images or videos that have been altered to mislead viewers about the original content.

Our Mission

CredGuard is committed to combating the spread of misinformation by providing cutting-edge AI-powered tools that help users identify fake content. Our advanced algorithms analyze text, images, and videos for signs of manipulation, ensuring you can trust what you see and read online.

By combining multiple detection methods and cross-referencing with reliable sources, we provide comprehensive analysis that empowers users to make informed decisions about the content they consume and share.

Why It Matters

In today's digital age, misinformation spreads faster than ever before. False information can influence public opinion, affect elections, damage reputations, and even cause harm to individuals and communities.

By using CredGuard, you become part of the solution. Together, we can create a more informed and trustworthy digital environment where facts matter and truth prevails.
